Module 1: Atmosphere Mechanics

The Recipe of Air

We often think air is just oxygen, but it is a specific mixture. If this balance breaks, we die.

  • Nitrogen (N2): 78% (Most abundant)
  • Oxygen (O2): 21% (Required for breathing & combustion)
  • Trace Gases: 1% (Argon, CO2, Water Vapor)

Module 2: Pollution Dynamics

Temperature Inversions

Normally, warm air rises and carries pollution away. In a "Heat Dome" (Inversion), a layer of warm air sits on top of cold air near the ground, acting like a LID. This traps smoke and smog at ground level.

The Toxic Six (Common Pollutants)

These are the 6 major pollutants monitored by the Council. Know your enemy.

  • 1. Particulate Matter (PM2.5): From wildfire smoke and dust. Lodges deep in lungs.
  • 2. Ground-Level Ozone (O3): Created by sun reacting with car exhaust. Causes "sunburn" on lungs.
  • 3. Nitrogen Oxides (NOx): From high-heat engines (diesel/gas). Creates acid rain.
  • 4. Sulfur Dioxide (SO2): From burning coal or diesel. Smells like burnt matches.
  • 5. Carbon Monoxide (CO): Odorless killer from incomplete combustion (generators).
  • 6. Hydrogen Sulfide (H2S): "Sour Gas" from rotting waste or oil/gas. Smells like rotten eggs.

Module 3: The Great Misunderstanding

Ozone Hole vs. Global Warming

These are two different problems. Don't confuse them!

Concept The Analogy Function
The Ozone Layer [Image of Sunglasses] Protects us from UV Rays (Sunburn/Cancer). Does NOT trap heat.
Greenhouse Gases [Image of Winter Coat] Traps heat (Infrared) to keep Earth warm. Too many layers = Overheating.

Module 4: Climate Detectives

Proxy Data: How do we know the past?

We use "Proxies" to measure climate from before we had thermometers.

  • Tree Rings: Wide Ring = Warm/Wet year. Narrow Ring = Cold/Dry year. [Image of tree rings climate]
  • Ice Cores: Tiny bubbles trapped in ancient ice contain old air. We analyze them to measure CO2 levels from 10,000 years ago.

Module 5: Stewardship & Survival Tech

Changing the Economy

  • Linear Economy: Take -> Make -> Waste. (Unsustainable).
  • Circular Economy: Take -> Make -> Reuse -> Return. (The goal of the Dome).

Indigenous Ways of Knowing: Focuses on stewardship and treating nature as a relative rather than a commodity to be sold.

Survival Tech Specs (For Roles 4, 6, 7)

  • Biogas Digester (Role 4): A tank that takes organic waste (food/poop) and uses bacteria to break it down. Output: Methane (fuel for cooking/heating) and Fertilizer (for crops).
  • Drought-Resistant GMOs (Role 7): Crops genetically modified to survive with 50% less water. Pros: Prevents starvation. Cons: Risk of reducing biodiversity or "superweeds."
  • Passive Solar Retrofitting (Role 6): Modifying existing buildings to save energy. Examples: Installing high-efficiency windows, adding insulation, and painting roofs white to reflect heat.

Training Manual: What is Air Quality?

Particulate Matter (PM2.5): These are tiny particles (dust, smoke) smaller than 2.5 microns. They can penetrate deep into lungs and enter the bloodstream. High PM2.5 causes asthma and heart issues.

Carbon Dioxide (CO2): Safe levels are ~400 ppm (parts per million). At 1000 ppm, people get headaches and drowsy. At 2000 ppm, cognitive function drops.

AQI (Air Quality Index): A scale from 1-10+ used in Canada. 1-3 is Low Risk, 7-10 is High Risk.

The Demographer

Focus: ES20-HP1 (Population Dynamics)

MISSION: The Dome was built for 500 people. We are over capacity. You must calculate growth rates and determine if we are experiencing exponential or logistic growth.
Training Manual: Population Math

Growth Rate (r): The speed at which a population grows.
Formula: r = (Births + Immigration) - (Deaths + Emigration).

Rule of 70: Estimating how many years it takes a population to double.
Formula: Doubling Time = 70 / growth rate (as a percentage).

Carrying Capacity (K): The maximum number of individuals the environment can support sustainably.

Demographic Transition Model (DTM): A model showing how countries change over time. Stage 1 (High Birth/Death) -> Stage 4 (Low Birth/Death). To lower birth rates, you need to improve education and healthcare.

Training Manual: Sustainable Agriculture

Monoculture: Growing only one crop (like wheat). It is efficient but risky (one bug can kill it all) and depletes soil.

Nitrogen Cycle: Plants need nitrogen. Bacteria in the soil fix nitrogen from the air. Over-farming kills these bacteria.

Aquifers: Underground water storage. If you pump water out faster than it rains (recharge), the aquifer dries up.
